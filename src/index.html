Wonderful moments of being a Mandarin teacher

Chinese painting workshop: plum blossom

It was the beginning of spring, and Year 4 students were learning colour vocabulary in Mandarin lessons. So I thought it would be great for the culture workshop's topic to focus on Chinese painting, where students could not only gain real-life experience in Chinese painting but also reinforce their memory of colour vocabulary.

Chinese New Year workshop: Calligraphy

This is Year 6 student Nitya's calligraphy work. During Chinese New Year celebrations, students learned about traditional customs and became intrigued by the character “福” (fú, meaning “luck”). I demonstrated how to write it, and afterward, students practiced creating their own versions of the character.

Chinese workshop: blue and white porcelain

After a school trip to the British Museum, students' interests in blue-and-white porcelain were piqued. I held a workshop to introduce it in detail, and students designed their own blue-and-white porcelain patterns on paper plates. Some even tried writing the Chinese characters for “blue and white porcelain” (青花瓷).

Chinese workshop: Beijing opera facial makeup

My students have a very deep appreciation for the story "Journey to the West". Their favourite figure has always been the Monkey King. After the session of Beijing Opera, I thought it would be so thrilling for my students that they will make a Beijing opera facial makeup for the Monkey King. My thought turned out to be true. They had a lot of fun.

Chinese traditional clothes workshop: Qipao

A group of my students watched a Chinese costume drama and enjoyed it. They asked why I never wore stylish clothes like the actresses. This inspired me to introduce Chinese traditional clothes to my students. When they were introduced to Qipao, a very well-known Chinese traditional clothing, they couldn't wait to design their versions of Qipao, hoping that they could make it real one day. I had fear that boys would't enjoy this workshop as much as girls did, but I was wrong, they told me that they wanted to make one for their moms. I found it so sweet and touching.
